Brief Timeline of Ottoman Empire History

Rising from a small Anatolian beylik founded by Osman I in 1299, the Ottoman Empire evolved into a vast intercontinental power spanning Europe, Asia, and Africa. Its defining triumph came in 1453 when Mehmed the Conqueror captured Constantinople, transforming the Byzantine capital into Istanbul – a dazzling hub of Islamic art and architecture. Under Süleyman the Magnificent (1520–1566), the empire reached its zenith: architects like Mimar Sinan redefined skylines with masterpieces like the Süleymaniye Mosque, while workshops from Iznik to Baghdad produced luminous ceramics and calligraphy that blended Persian, Arab, and Turkic traditions.

Yet after 1683, military defeats and internal struggles triggered decline. Reforms like the Tanzimat (1839–1876) modernized institutions but couldn’t halt fragmentation. Following WWI defeat, the empire dissolved in 1922–1923, giving birth to modern Turkey. For over 600 years, it shaped global politics, trade, and culture – leaving behind minaret-studded horizons, illuminated Qurans, and a legacy of synthesis where Byzantine domes met Ottoman tiles in timeless harmony.

1. Foundation and Rise (1299-1453)

Mehmed II, known as "the Conqueror," captured Constantinople in 1453, ending the Byzantine Empire.

  • 1299-1326: Osman I establishes the Ottoman Beylik. The Ottomans' first major conquests are in Anatolia, securing their base against the Byzantine Empire. Osman's leadership sets the foundation for a new dynasty.

  • 1326-1359: Orhan Gazi consolidates power. Bursa is captured and becomes the first capital. The Ottomans cross into Europe, marking the start of their Balkan expansion.

  • 1359-1389: Murad I's conquests and institutions. Murad I captures Adrianople and makes it the new capital. The Janissary Corps is established. The Battle of Kosovo in 1389 solidifies Ottoman control in the Balkans.

  • 1389-1402: Bayezid I's rapid expansion. The Ottoman military defeats the Crusaders at the Battle of Nicopolis in 1396, establishing the empire as a major European power.

  • 1402-1413: Ottoman Interregnum. A period of civil war after Bayezid's defeat by Timur. The empire is in chaos until Mehmed I restores order.

  • 1413-1451: Mehmed I and Murad II. Mehmed I rebuilds the state, and Murad II expands the empire further.

  • 1453: Fall of Constantinople. Mehmed II the Conqueror captures Constantinople, making it the new Ottoman capital. The conquest marks the end of the Byzantine Empire and the beginning of the empire's golden age.
