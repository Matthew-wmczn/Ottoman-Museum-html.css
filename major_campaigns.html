The history of the Ottoman Empire is inseparable from its military campaigns, which shaped its borders, power, and legacy over more than six centuries. From its early days as a small Anatolian beylik, the empire expanded through decisive battles and strategic sieges, forging a realm that spanned Europe, Asia, and Africa. Victories such as Varna, Constantinople, and Mohács cemented Ottoman dominance, while campaigns at Vienna and Lepanto tested the limits of its reach. These conflicts were more than clashes of armies—they were turning points that influenced trade, religion, diplomacy, and the fate of entire regions. The Ottoman military adapted across eras, from the horse-archer tactics of the 14th century to the gunpowder sieges of the 15th, the naval dominance of the 16th, and the trench warfare of the 20th. Yet alongside triumph came defeat, reform, and eventual dissolution. Each campaign tells a story of ambition, resilience, and transformation, charting the rise, peak, and fall of one of history’s most enduring empires.

1. Rise and Expansion (1300s–1500s)

Mehmed ll, Entering the City of Constantinople

  • Battle of Kosovo (1389) – Secured Ottoman dominance in the Balkans despite the death of Sultan Murad I, paving the way for future expansion.

  • Battle of Nicopolis (1396) – A crushing victory over a Crusader army, showcasing Ottoman tactical superiority against European knights.

  • Battle of Varna (1444) – Defeated the final major Crusader coalition in Eastern Europe, solidifying Ottoman supremacy in the Balkans.

  • Fall of Constantinople (1453) – Mehmed II’s siege and massive “Basilica” cannon ended the Byzantine Empire, making Istanbul the new imperial capital.

  • Battle of Otlukbeli (1473) – Defeated the rival Akkoyunlu Turkmen, securing eastern Anatolia from Persian influence.

  • Battle of Mohács (1526) – Decisive victory over Hungary, splitting the kingdom and opening the way to Vienna.

  • First Siege of Vienna (1529) – Failed to capture the Habsburg capital but cemented Ottoman presence deep in Central Europe.

2. Peak & Challenges (1550s–1700s)

A paint of 1683 second siege of vienna in Heeresgeschichtlichen Museum Wien

  • Battle of Djerba (1560) – Naval victory over Spain and its allies, securing Ottoman supremacy in the central Mediterranean.

  • Siege of Cyprus (1570–1571) – Captured the island from Venice, though victory was overshadowed by naval defeat at Lepanto.

  • Battle of Lepanto (1571) – Holy League’s naval win broke Ottoman myth of invincibility at sea, though the fleet was rebuilt quickly.

  • Long Turkish War (1593–1606) – Prolonged conflict with the Habsburgs drained resources but preserved most Ottoman frontiers.

  • Battle of Khotyn (1621) – Fought to a costly stalemate with the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth, halting northern expansion.

  • Second Siege of Vienna (1683) – A decisive defeat at the hands of a Polish-led coalition, marking the beginning of territorial retreat in Europe.

  • Great Turkish War (1683–1699) – Series of defeats against the Holy League culminating in the Treaty of Karlowitz, the first major Ottoman land loss.

3. Reform, Decline & Final Wars (1700s–1922)

ANZAC Landing at Gallipoli

  • Russo–Turkish War (1768–1774) – Ended in defeat and loss of Crimea, exposing Ottoman military weakness to European powers.

  • Napoleonic Campaign in Egypt & Syria (1798–1801) – Ottomans, aided by Britain, expelled French forces, showing reliance on European allies.

  • Greek War of Independence (1821–1829) – Naval defeat at Navarino and loss of Greece signaled decline in Ottoman control over Christian provinces.

  • Crimean War (1853–1856) – Victory with British and French support against Russia, but logistical flaws were revealed.

  • Balkan Wars (1912–1913) – Rapid losses to Balkan states cost nearly all European territories.

  • Gallipoli Campaign (1915–1916) – Successful defense against Allied landings in WWI, elevating Mustafa Kemal as a national hero.

  • Arab Revolt (1916–1918) – Uprisings backed by the British severed Ottoman control in the Middle East, accelerating collapse.

  • Dissolution & Birth of the Republic (1922–1923) – After WWI defeat and Allied occupation, the sultanate was abolished; the Turkish War of Independence led to the proclamation of the Republic of Turkey under Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.
